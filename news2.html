Reduction of working hours in Colombia is already law

July 16, 2021 Carlos López

On Thursday afternoon was signed by the President of the Senate, Arturo Char, and by the Minister of Labor, Ángel Custodio Cabrera, the weekly labor reduction law approved last month in Congress; and, finally, the President of the Republic, Iván Duque approved it and it was stipulated as Law 2101 of July 15, 2021.

It will gradually reduce the weekly working hours of Colombians, "without reducing wages or affecting workers' acquired rights and guarantees," he says in his first article.

The authorship of the Law was in charge of the former senator Álvaro Uribe Vélez, who before his resignation to Congress raised the proposal to dismunuir, progressively, the working day from 48 to 42 hours per week. In its passage and vote by the legislature, it received wide support. In fact, in its last debate in the House of Representatives, it obtained 117 votes in favour and eight against.



This Act will amend article 161 of the Substantive Labour Code. The 42 hours of work reached must be completed, according to an agreement between the employer and employee, in five or six days. Always guaranteed one day off a week.

And there are other agreements you can reach between you. One of these concerns flexibility of work, since weekly hours must be met "with a minimum of four continuous hours and a maximum of up to nine hours per day without any additional work.", is noted in the document.
